Scientific Supplement Ledger

Unbiased dosage instructions, efficacy reports, and industry myths vs facts

Efficacy Profiles

Whey Protein Isolate / Concentrate
Purpose: Ultra fast muscle protein synthesis optimization.

Optimal Dose: 25-30g post-training or morning window.

Note: Whey is simply an extremely convenient high-quality complete protein source extracted from milk. It is not an artificial compound.
Creatine Monohydrate
Purpose: Adenosine Triphosphate (ATP) energy pathway saturation to increase muscular power output.

Optimal Dose: 3-5g daily consistently. No loading phase strictly required.

Note: Creatine is the most heavily researched athletic supplement globally. Ensure you increase your daily water hydration bounds.
Beta-Alanine (Pre-workout)
Purpose: Muscle carnosine saturation to buffer lactic acid formation during intensive sets.

Optimal Dose: 2-5g pre-training.

Note: May cause a harmless tingling sensation (paresthesia) across surface capillary boundaries.

Truth vs Fiction

Myth: Supplements damage renal/kidney structures.

Fact: Peer-reviewed research proves Creatine and Whey do not strain healthy kidneys when daily fluid protocols are maintained.

Myth: Mass gainers are superior for size building.

Fact: Most mass gainers are loaded with processed maltodextrin/sugars. Clean carbohydrate sources paired with protein are highly recommended instead.
